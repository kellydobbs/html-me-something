5 Totally Rad Things That Have Happened in 2020

Alright LaunchCoders, listen up. I know what you're thinking: "2020 has been a dumpster fire of a year." But it hasn't been all bad! Take a look at some of the best things that have happened in 2020 that you might have forgotten about.



Adam Castillejo Became the Second Person Ever to be Cured of HIV

Adam Castillejo, a London resident first diagnosed with HIV in 2003, became the second person to ever be cured of the virus. Castillejo has been experiencing long-term remission following a special bone-marrow transplant. He has now been free of HIV for 34 months and no logner requires antiretroviral therapy, according to The Lancet journal. An estimate of 37.9 million people live with HIV globally.



Diego Returns Home

Diego, a 100 year-old Hood Island giant tortoise credited with saving his species, was returned home to the uninhabited island of Española Island, one of the Galápagos Islands. Diego was first captured and shipped to an American zoo sometime between 1928-1933. Diego later spent decades in a breeding program on Santa Cruz Island beginning in 1977 until he was finally retired in January of this year. He has fathered an estimated 900 offspring and saved his species from extinction.

Costa Rica Legalized Same-Sex Marriage

Costa Rica became the first country in Central America to legalize sam-sex marraige. On May 26th, Alexandra Quiros and Dunia Araya because the first same-sex couple to marry under the new law. Costa Rica's constitutation court ruled that a ban on same-sex marriage was unconsittuional in August 2018. Today, same-sex marriages are now legal in 29 countries.
